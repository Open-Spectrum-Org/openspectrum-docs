To make this truly indispensable for parents, the app needs to move beyond a simple "log" and become a tool that reduces their mental load. Since you want this to be frictionless and open source, the features should focus on speed, community-driven insights, and data sovereignty.

Here are 10 features to brainstorm:

### 1. The "Hot Key" Voice Memo
Instead of navigating menus, parents should be able to long-press a button (or use a lock-screen widget) to record a quick audio snippet. The app should use AI to automatically parse that audio and categorize it into **Food**, **Meds**, or **Behavior**.

### 2. "Incident" Tagging with One Tap
Parents often need to track "meltdowns" or "stimming" episodes. A simple "Quick Tag" system where a parent can tap a single icon to timestamp an event allows them to log the behavior instantly and add details later when they have more time.

### 3. Smart Correlation Engine
This is where the "harnessing" begins. The app could look for patterns and provide subtle nudges:
* "I noticed a spike in hyperactivity on days when [Medication X] was taken after 4 PM."
* "Sleep quality improved by 20% on days with no dairy."

### 4. Shared Caretaker Sync
A child often has a "village"—parents, teachers, therapists, and grandparents. This feature would allow the primary user to grant "Contributor" access to others, so the school can log a behavior and the parent sees it instantly on their dashboard.

### 5. PDF "Doctor's Report" Generator
One of the biggest pain points is explaining the last 3 months to a pediatrician or neurologist in a 15-minute appointment. A single button that compiles all logs into a clean, professional PDF summary would be a massive "killer feature."

### 6. Anonymous Benchmarking (The "Comparison" Feature)
Using the open-source community data, parents could opt-in to see how their child’s progress compares to others in the same "cohort" (e.g., "Non-verbal, age 5-7"). This provides a sense of community and helps set realistic expectations.

### 7. Medication & Supplement Reminders
A robust notification system for dosing schedules that also asks for a "Quick React" 30 minutes later: "How is their focus now? (High/Normal/Low)." This ties the medication directly to the behavioral outcome.

### 8. Sensory Environment Logger
Using the phone’s sensors, the app could automatically log environmental data like **noise levels (dB)** or **light intensity** during a logged behavioral event, helping to identify external triggers that the parent might have missed.

### 9. Milestone "Victory" Gallery
Autism parenting can be exhausting. Having a dedicated space to log "Small Wins"—like a new word or a successful transition—ensures the data isn't just about "problems," but also about celebrating progress.

### 10. Local-First Privacy Mode
To truly respect the "Open" and "PII" requirements, the app could function entirely offline, storing data only on the parent's device. They would have to explicitly click "Sync to Cloud" or "Share with Community" to move data off their phone, giving them total control.

---

### A Quick Consideration for the Open Source Aspect
To attract developers, you could build a **Plugin Architecture**. This would allow the community to build their own "Modules" (e.g., a "GFCF Diet Tracker" or a "Sleep Study Module") that parents can toggle on or off based on their specific needs.
